Introduction

Marine pollution refers to the introduction of harmful substances such as plastic, chemicals, and waste into the ocean. These pollutants have detrimental effects on marine ecosystems and biodiversity.

Causes of Waste in Marine Environments

  • Plastic Waste
  • Oil Spills
  • Sewage and Wastewater Discharge
  • Industrial and Agricultural Runoff

Impact on Marine Life

Marine waste affects wildlife by disrupting habitats, causing injuries, and leading to the ingestion of toxic materials. Marine life such as fish, birds, and mammals are at risk of death or harm due to entanglement and ingestion of plastics.

Solutions

  • Reduce Plastic Usage
  • Implement Proper Waste Management
  • Promote Ocean Clean-up Initiatives
  • Increase Public Awareness
